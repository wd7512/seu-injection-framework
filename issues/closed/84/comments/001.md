WITHDRAWN — This whole group was a mistake. 

The bitflip time (~3μs) and GPU transfer (~5μs) are both <0.01% of the per-iteration time, which is dominated by the forward pass (1-100ms). The 1,176× speedup number on the vectorized path is technically true but practically meaningless — the bottleneck is 4+ orders of magnitude larger.

Worse, the proposed fix ("flip all bits at once, do one forward pass") fundamentally misunderstands exhaustive SEU injection. The whole point of the algorithm is to measure each individual bitflip's impact. You cannot batch them.

The only real optimization (staying on-GPU via tensor.view(torch.int32) instead of the CPU numpy round trip) saves <0.01% — not worth an issue.

Apologies for the noise.
